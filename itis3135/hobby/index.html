The Essence of HCI

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) is a multidisciplinary field focusing on the design of computer technology and the interaction between humans and users.

Core Pillar Description
Usability Ensuring systems are efficient and easy to learn.
Accessibility Making tech usable for everyone, regardless of ability.
User Experience Focusing on the emotional and meaningful journey of a user.

Optimal Research Cycles

The "When" of HCI is constant. Research should begin before the first line of code is written and continue through the maintenance phase of a product.

Key phases include the Discovery Phase, Iterative Testing, and Post-Launch Analytics.

Prompt: A glowing, futuristic horizontal timeline showing phases like Discover, Define, Ideate, and Prototype, photorealistic, blurred background.

The Lab and the Field

HCI takes place in a variety of environments. While labs provide controlled data, "in-the-wild" testing reveals how users behave in stressful or distracting real-world scenarios.

  • Usability Testing Labs
  • Contextual Inquiry (Home/Office)
  • Remote Testing (Digital)

The Methodology

We use a variety of tools and methods to measure interaction quality, ranging from eye-tracking software to simple paper prototypes.

Why HCI Matters

Technology is now an essential part of the human experience. If tech is hard to use, it creates a digital divide. HCI seeks to close that gap.

By studying how people think (cognition) and feel (emotion), we can build tools that truly empower rather than frustrate.
